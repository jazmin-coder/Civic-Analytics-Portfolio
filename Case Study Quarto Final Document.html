How Can a Wellness Technology Company Play It Smart?

Introduction

The executive team from Bellabeat, a high-tech manufacturer of health-focused products for women, wants to understand consumer usage of smart devices. By understanding the primary uses and trends of smart devices, Bellabeat can effectively market its products by focusing on specific goals or benefits that resonate with potential customers across markets. This approach can help the company save money, attract new customers, retain existing ones, and increase membership revenue.

The data collected from these smart devices include activity levels, sleep patterns, stress, and reproductive health of women. Analyzing this data will help us identify our target consumers and gain a deeper understanding of their needs.

Problem: The company is seeking additional opportunities for growth by leveraging targeted, data-driven marketing strategies.

Business Task: In this project, I’m a junior data analyst working for Bellabeat. It is a small company, but it has the potential to grow into a competitive player in the global smart device market. The company wants me to figure out how consumers typically use their smart fitness devices and develop some high-level recommendations. The insights acquired from this analysis will help the marketing team develop better targeted marketing strategies, and consumers will benefit from tailored offerings that align with their interests.

Prepare

The data I’ll use to answer the business task comes from a survey of thirty Fitbit users. I used datasets on their daily activities from March to May 2016 (Fitbit Fitness Tracker Data), which was open-source data downloaded from Kaggle.

The data from both data sets were organized in a long format, which is useful for viewing how the data trends over time. There doesn’t seem to be any immediately noticeable issue with bias or credibility. The data is directly collected from the measurements the devices make while worn. And the thirty eligible Fitbit users consented to the submission of personal tracker data, including minute-level output for physical activity, heart rate, and sleep monitoring. The tool I used to import and analyze the data was RStudio and the R programming language. The data files contained a lot of information, so R could easily handle processing a lot of that data with simple commands. To ensure data integrity, the data was left as it appeared in the data file, with only a few transformations to ease the process of data visualization. I filtered out erroneous data points that provided no relevant information or made no sense, which were likely due to an error in the monitoring equipment. The following is the step-by-step process I followed to ensure the data was clean and transformed for the visualization.

Conclusion

As we can see with the generated combined scatter plot and line graph, consumers generally use their smart devices for tracking their physical activity progress. The data points represent the distance traveled by all users in proportion to the calories they lost on a day to day basis between the months of March to May in 2016. There is a positive correlation between physical activity and the calories expended, which was statistically significant, as seen in the calculated pearson correlation.

The information presented this way is useful because it can be used to persuade potential consumers interested in losing weight and becoming more physically active. Being able to track your actual progress with a smart device is more conducive towards actually meeting your goals. The Bellabeat product could be marketed as a light, wearable tool that will encourage you to move more by presenting you with information on your current state. There could even be an added feature where the tool can send you reminders to meet your daily goals or to move more often if sedentary. The selling point of Bellabeat is helping users to meet their health goals, and what better way to do it than having tangible results on the palm of your hand (literally).

For future research, I would like to investigate the effects of physical activity on sleeping patterns. Could this also be potential benefit for users who would like to track and better their sleeping habits?
